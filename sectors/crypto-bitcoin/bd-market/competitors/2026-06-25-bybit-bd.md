## Strengths

1. **Derivatives leader** — Bybit is the #2 crypto derivatives exchange globally, offering up to 100x leverage. BD traders actively use it for futures trading.
2. **P2P BDT support** — Bybit P2P marketplace supports BDT via bKash, Nagad, and bank transfers, making it a direct competitor to Binance for BD on/off ramp.
3. **Copy trading** — Bybit's copy trading feature lets BD users follow experienced traders, reducing the learning curve for newcomers.
4. **Low fees** — 0.1% spot trading fee (0.075% with BGB) vs Binance's 0.1%. Maker rebates on futures.
5. **Strong BD community** — Active Bangladesh Telegram and Facebook groups dedicated to Bybit trading signals and strategies.

## Weaknesses

1. **No official BD presence** — No registered office in Bangladesh. Customer support is global, not localized. No Bengali language support.
2. **Derivatives risk** — High leverage trading leads to frequent retail losses. Reputation hit in BD when local traders lose funds.
3. **P2P scam exposure** — Bybit's P2P platform, like Binance, faces scam risk. BD users report frozen funds during P2P disputes.
4. **Regulatory uncertainty** — No clarity on Bangladesh Bank's stance on Bybit specifically. Potential for ISP blocking as seen in other markets.
5. **Less brand trust** — In BD, Binance dominates brand recognition. Bybit is perceived as a "derivatives exchange" not a general crypto platform.

---

## Threat Level: High

Bybit directly competes with Binance for BD P2P users and futures traders. Its copy trading feature attracts new entrants who might otherwise use a local crypto platform. Low fees and BDT P2P make it a strong #2 in BD.
