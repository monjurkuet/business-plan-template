## Strengths

1. **Altcoin selection** — KuCoin lists more small-cap and emerging altcoins than Binance or Bybit, making it the go-to platform for BD traders seeking new tokens.
2. **Low minimums** — No minimum deposit for most coins. BD users can start trading with as little as $10 USDT, lowering the barrier to entry.
3. **KuCoin Earn** — Staking, lending, and fixed-income products attract BD users looking for passive yield on their crypto holdings.
4. **BD Telegram community** — Active BD-specific signal groups and trading communities built around KuCoin's altcoin listings.
5. **No KYC for withdrawals** — KuCoin allows withdrawals up to 1 BTC daily without full KYC, appealing to privacy-conscious BD users.

## Weaknesses

1. **Higher scam token exposure** — KuCoin lists more unvetted tokens. BD users have been caught in rug pulls on low-cap KuCoin listings.
2. **BDT on/off ramp limited** — No direct BDT P2P marketplace. BD users must first acquire USDT on Binance/Bybit P2P, then transfer to KuCoin.
3. **Lower liquidity** — For most altcoin pairs, liquidity is significantly lower than Binance. Slippage is an issue for BD traders executing large orders.
4. **Reputation risk** — KuCoin has been hacked ($280M in 2020, partially reimbursed). "KuCoin hack" is still a common BD search term.
5. **No bangla support** — English-only interface and customer support. Less accessible to Bengali-only speakers.

---

## Threat Level: Medium

KuCoin targets a specific niche — BD altcoin traders and yield seekers. It doesn't compete for the general BD crypto user because of the BDT on/off ramp gap. However, for crypto-savvy BD users, it's an essential complement to Binance.
