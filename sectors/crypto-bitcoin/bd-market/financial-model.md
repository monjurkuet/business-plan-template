## Key Assumptions

1. **Regulatory clarity:** Assumes crypto trading remains legal in Bangladesh. Any explicit ban or banking restriction would make P2P/OTC revenue streams impossible without workarounds.
2. **Volume growth:** Assumes Bangladesh P2P volume grows at 20–25% CAGR (aligned with global adoption index). A plateau would slow user acquisition.
3. **Fee compression:** Competitive pressure from Binance P2P (already dominant) limits fee upside. Assumes we can sustain 0.8% avg fee via superior UX and local support.
4. **CAC efficiency:** Organic growth via Telegram/Discord referrals assumed for 60% of users. If paid channels dominate, blended CAC could rise to ৳400+.
5. **Bank/MFS integration:** bKash/Nagad on-ramps assumed available. Any restrictions by MFS providers on crypto-adjacent transactions would cripple P2P volume.
6. **No proprietary trading risk:** Model is agency-only (fee revenue). No balance-sheet crypto exposure assumed.
7. **Security cost escalation:** Cyber threats assumed managed within budget. A major breach would require ৳2–5M emergency response.
8. **Education conversion:** Assumes 15% of education course buyers convert to platform traders. Below 8% conversion, education becomes a loss leader.
9. **Stablecoin reliance:** Most volume assumed in USDT. A stablecoin de-pegging event (like UST 2022) would destroy trading volume for 2–3 months.
10. **RISKIEST assumption:** Regulatory stability. A Supreme Court petition or Bangladesh Bank circular restricting crypto could make the entire model invalid overnight.
