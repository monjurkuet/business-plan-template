# Landscape Report — Logistics & Transport

## Sector Overview

Bangladesh's logistics sector is estimated at ~$20B (2025) growing at 8–10% CAGR. The courier/parcel segment alone is ~3,000 Cr growing at 15–18% annually, driven by e-commerce. Truck transport (freight) is a 15,000+ Cr market with 80%+ informal penetration.

## Infrastructure Bottlenecks

1. **Road congestion:** Dhaka (~50% of freight spends 2–4× time in traffic vs distance)
2. **Bangabandhu Bridge & Padma Bridge:** Reduced transit time across river; still bottleneck during peak
3. **Chittagong Port dwell time:** Average 7–10 days container dwell (target: <5)
4. **Warehouse density:** Only 6–8% formal warehousing vs 40%+ needed in urban areas
5. **Last-mile infrastructure:** Vehicle restriction (3-wheeler bans) in some Dhaka routes

## Key Trends

1. **E-commerce logistics:** 25%+ YoY growth in parcel volumes; delivery time expectations compressing (from 5 days to 24–48h)
2. **Tech integration:** API-connected logistics (Kuri, RedX, Paperfly offering Shopify/WooCommerce plugins)
3. **Cold chain expansion:** IMED (Bangladesh) + Tolaram investing in cold storage
4. **Cross-border logistics:** Growing Bangladesh–India–Nepal–Bhutan connectivity (BBIN MVA)
5. **Barge/river freight:** Alternative to road freight; 20–30% cheaper per ton-km
6. **Government digitization:** National Single Window (NSW) for customs; E-Permit systems

## Entry Strategy Recommendation

- **Phase 1:** Niche last-mile delivery for e-commerce within Dhaka metro (tech-first, real-time tracking)
- **Phase 2:** Add warehousing + fulfillment service (subscription storage + pick-pack-ship)
- **Phase 3:** Intercity freight brokerage (digital freight matching — "Uber for trucks")
