# Regulatory — Agriculture & Agro-processing

## Required Registrations

| Requirement | Authority | Cost (BDT) | Timeline |
|------------|-----------|:---------:|----------|
| Trade License | City Corporation / Union Parishad | 5,000–15,000 | 7–14 days |
| VAT Registration (if turnover > 30L) | NBR | Free (BIN) | 14–30 days |
| TIN Certificate | NBR | Free | 7 days |
| BSTI Quality Mark (processed foods) | BSTI | 10,000–50,000 | 30–90 days |
| Food Safety License | BFSA (Bangladesh Food Safety Authority) | 5,000–20,000 | 30–60 days |
| Environmental Clearance | DoE | 2,000–10,000 | 30–60 days |
| Fire License | Fire Service & Civil Defence | 2,000–5,000 | 14–30 days |
| Export Registration Certificate (ERC) | CCI&E | 1,000–2,500 | 7–14 days |

## Sector-Specific Regulations

| Regulation | Details |
|-----------|---------|

| BSTI standards for agro products | Quality specifications for rice, pulses, oilseeds, spices |
| Plant Quarantine Act, 2011 | Phytosanitary requirements for export |
| Food Safety Act, 2013 (BFSA) | Food safety and hygiene standards |
| Seed Act, 2018 (Ministry of Agriculture) | Seed quality certification |

## Tax Obligations

| Tax Type | Rate | Notes |
|----------|:---:|-------|
| Corporate Tax (agro-processing) | 25–32.5% | Depending on incorporation type |
| VAT on processed foods | 5–15% | Reduced for some essential items |
| Source Tax on supply contracts | 5% | Deducted at source |
| Customs Duty (export-oriented) | 0–5% | Bonded warehouse benefits available |

## Import/Export Compliance

- Phytosanitary certificate from Plant Quarantine Wing (DAE) for all plant exports
- Halal certification for export to Middle East ($500–2,000, Islamic Foundation Bangladesh)
- BSTI certificate for export quality assurance
- EUR.1/Form A for EU GSP preferences
