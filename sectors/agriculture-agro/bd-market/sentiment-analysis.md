# Sentiment Analysis — Agriculture & Agro-processing

## Customer Satisfaction Themes

| Theme | Signal | Sentiment | Evidence |
|-------|--------|:---------:|----------|
| Quality consistency | Top complaint: varying quality between batches | 🟡 Mixed | Common issue with smallholder supply chains |
| Price predictability | Buyers prefer fixed-rate contracts | 🟢 Positive | Wholesalers value stable pricing |
| Delivery reliability | Transport disruptions common in monsoon | 🟡 Mixed | Infrastructure limitation |
| Traceability | Premium buyers willing to pay 10–15% more for traceable origin | 🟢 Positive | Export markets (ME/EU) demand this |
| Freshness guarantee | Restaurants prefer guaranteed freshness windows | 🟢 Positive | High willingness-to-pay |
| Payment terms | Farmers complain about delayed payments (30–60 days) | 🔴 Negative | Systemic issue in Bangladeshi agro supply chain |
| Organic certification | Growing demand but supply limited | 🟢 Positive | 30% YoY growth (Dhaka premium segment) |

## Voice of Customer (Common Complaints)

1. **"Guner maal ekbar ashse, porer bar different"** — Quality inconsistency across batches
2. **"Delivery always late during rain"** — Monsoon logistics failures
3. **"Farmers don't get fair price"** — Middlemen capture 50–60% of retail price
4. **"No guarantee on pesticide levels"** — Health-conscious buyers fear contamination
5. **"Export-grade quality hard to source consistently"** — Processors struggle with supply stability

## Improvement Levers

1. **Quality grading (A/B/C tiers)** — Transparent grading system with BSTI lab backing → +0.5–1.0 rating point
2. **7-day farmer payment guarantee** — Direct deposit via bKash/Nagad → farmer retention + supplier loyalty
3. **Monsoon-proof logistics** — Covered transport + Dhaka warehouse buffer stock → delivery reliability
4. **QR traceability** — Consumer-facing origin tracking → premium segment conversion
