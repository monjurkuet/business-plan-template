# Landscape Report — Agriculture & Agro-processing

## Sector Overview

Agriculture employs ~40% of Bangladesh's workforce and contributes ~12% to GDP (FY2024–25 estimate). Agro-processing is a rapidly growing sub-sector driven by export demand, domestic retail modernization, and government incentives.

## Value Chain

```
Inputs (seed, fertilizer, pesticide) → Farmer/Cultivator → Local Aratdar (commission agent)
→ Wholesaler (Bepari) → Arat (wholesale market) → Retailer/Processor → Consumer/Export
```

**Typical margin distribution:** Farmer (15–20%) → Aratdar (3–5%) → Bepari (5–8%) → Wholesaler (8–12%) → Retailer (15–25%)

## Growth Drivers

1. **Export diversification:** Bangladesh targeting $5B agro export by 2030 (current ~$1.2B)
2. **Cold chain investment:** Govt + private investment (Bangladesh Bank green refinancing scheme)
3. **Organic/demand premium:** Dhaka metro consumers willing to pay 20–30% premium for traceable, pesticide-free produce
4. **E-commerce agro:** Chaldal, MeenaClick, direct farm-to-consumer models growing at 15–20% YoY
5. **Rice fortification mandate:** Government program creating demand for processed rice

## Key Trends

- AI/image-based crop disease detection startups entering (iFarmer, Shuddho)
- Contract farming increasing (Pran, Square contracting 50,000+ farmers)
- Bangladesh-India agro trade corridor growing ($1.2B+ bilateral agro trade)
- Climate adaptation: salt-tolerant rice varieties, floating farms in flood-prone Haor regions

## Entry Strategy Recommendation

- **Phase 1:** Build a 50-supplier directory (rice mills, poultry farms, vegetable aggregators) in 3 regions (Bogra, Jessore, Comilla)
- **Phase 2:** Supply Dhaka institutional buyers (hotels, restaurants, university dining) at competitive prices with consistent quality
- **Phase 3:** Add export aggregation (Middle East, EU organic certification track)
