# Risk Register — BIM Global Outreach (iNCOMPLEX)

## Risk Matrix

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|------|:---:|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | Low response rate from cold outreach (0.5–2% typical) | High | High | A/B test messaging, refine ICP, use multi-channel (LI + email + Clutch) |
| 2 | Price competition from India/Philippines BIM firms | High | Medium | Differentiate on architecture expertise + English communication; avoid race-to-bottom pricing |
| 3 | Quality inconsistency in BIM deliverables | Medium | Critical | Define LOD standards per project, implement internal QA/QC checklist, assign dedicated BIM manager per client |
| 4 | Client IP/NDA concerns (Western firms) | Medium | High | Standard NDA template, data security protocols, ISO 27001 alignment if needed |
| 5 | Timezone coordination friction | Medium | Medium | Establish 4-hour overlap window, clear communication protocol, asynchronous tools (BIM 360, Teams) |
| 6 | US/UK building code unfamiliarity | Medium | High | Invest in code research, hire local code consultant for first 3 projects, use third-party code review |
| 7 | Over-reliance on single market (US) | Medium | Medium | Develop UK/Australia/UAE channels in parallel |
| 8 | Autodesk/Bentley software licensing costs | Medium | Low | Start with Revit LT, scale to AEC Collection; pass software costs to client projects |
| 9 | Team burnout from fast-turnaround projects | Medium | High | 80% capacity cap, 2-3 week buffer on all schedules, never overcommit |
| 10 | Payment delays (30–90 day invoice terms) | High | Medium | 50% upfront for new clients, net 15–30 terms, invoice factoring as last resort |

## Kill Criteria

**Immediate kill signals:**
- 3 consecutive months without a closed deal from any channel
- Average deal size below $1,000/month (not sustainable)
- Negative client feedback on quality (2+ complaints)
- Cash flow gap exceeding 3 months

**Pause signals:**
- Response rate below 0.5% for 2 months → refine ICP and messaging
- Quality issues on 3+ projects → pause new signups, fix QA
- Timezone/communication friction on 3 projects → revise staffing model

## Key Assumptions

1. Target firms (20–500 employees) are overloaded enough to outsource
2. Architecture + MEP expertise is a genuine differentiator vs. generic BIM firms
3. US/UK/EU firms will trust BD-based team at comparable quality
4. $50–$150/hr rates are achievable with consultative positioning (vs. $25–50/hr for commodity BIM)
5. Consultative outreach will outperform transaction-based pitching
