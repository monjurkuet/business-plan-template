## Key Trends

1. **Fashion/imitation jewellery exploding via social media** — Instagram and Facebook shops sell imitation pieces at 70-80% margin. No trust barrier (it's not gold), impulse purchases drive volume.

2. **Gold price volatility pushes lighter pieces** — As gold prices rise, customers shift to lighter, machine-made pieces rather than heavy hand-crafted sets. This opens the market for design-first positioning.

3. **Hallmarked/BIS-standard gold gaining trust** — Consumers increasingly want certified gold. This is a trust signal that online-first brands can leverage.

4. **Silver jewellery growing among Gen-Z** — Cultural shift: younger buyers don't need gold as investment. Silver is fashionable, affordable, and Instagram-friendly.

5. **Lab-grown diamonds emerging** — Very early stage in BD, but globally growing 20%+. Opportunity for first-mover positioning in the BD market.

6. **Jewellery rental/subscription** — Weddings and Eid events require expensive sets used once. Rental models exist informally but no organized brand.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Start with fashion/imitation jewellery on Instagram** — Lowest trust barrier, highest margin, fastest to validate
2. **Build trust through transparency** — Video of packaging, weight verification, clear return policy
3. **Target wedding-adjacent market** — Not the bridal gold set (too trust-dependent), but matching accessories, bangles, tikli — the impulse purchases around the wedding
4. **Pivot to silver or lab-grown once brand is established** — Use imitation revenue to fund entry into higher-trust categories
5. **Explore rental model** — Massive opportunity for organized jewellery rental for events
