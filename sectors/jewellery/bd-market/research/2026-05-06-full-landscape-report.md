    35|
    36|1. **Fashion/imitation jewellery exploding via social media** — Instagram and Facebook shops sell imitation pieces at 70-80% margin. No trust barrier (it's not gold), impulse purchases drive volume.
    37|
    38|2. **Gold price volatility pushes lighter pieces** — As gold prices rise, customers shift to lighter, machine-made pieces rather than heavy hand-crafted sets. This opens the market for design-first positioning.
    39|
    40|3. **Hallmarked/BIS-standard gold gaining trust** — Consumers increasingly want certified gold. This is a trust signal that online-first brands can leverage.
    41|
    42|4. **Silver jewellery growing among Gen-Z** — Cultural shift: younger buyers don't need gold as investment. Silver is fashionable, affordable, and Instagram-friendly.

    46|6. **Jewellery rental/subscription** — Weddings and Eid events require expensive sets used once. Rental models exist informally but no organized brand.

## Verified Market Data (2024–2025)

### Market Sizing
- Total jewellery market: **$4–5B USD** (BAJUS estimate ~$4.5B)
- Gold accounts for ~60% of market by value
- Silver and fashion jewellery: growing segment (~$800M–1B)
- Gold demand: 20–25 tonnes/year (World Gold Council)
- Average gold purchase per household: ~$500–1,000/year in middle class

### Growth Drivers
- Cultural significance of gold in Bangladeshi weddings and festivals
- Gold price appreciation drives nominal market growth
- Hallmarking regulation boosting organized retail share
- Rising middle class → more discretionary spend on jewellery

*Sources: BAJUS 2024, World Gold Council 2024, The Daily Star 2024*
