    36|## Key Trends
    37|
    38|1. **D2C explosion via Instagram/Facebook** — New brands launch weekly with zero physical presence. The barrier to entry is a phone, a supplier, and a Facebook page.
    39|
    40|2. **Modest fashion is massively underserved** — Bangladesh is a majority-Muslim country, yet few brands do modest fashion well beyond basic hijab lines. There's a gap for curated, trendy modest wear.
    41|
    42|3. **Print-on-demand emerging** — Zero inventory model: customer orders → print → ship. Currently small but growing as local print partners emerge.
    43|
    44|4. **Live commerce via Reels** — Facebook/Instagram Reels are becoming the primary product discovery channel. Brands that master live selling outperform static posters.
    45|
    46|5. **Sustainable fashion niche** — Small but growing among affluent Gen-Z. Refashion, secondhand, and eco-friendly materials are gaining interest.
    47|
    48|6. **Custom tailoring digitization** — 3D body scan apps and online measurement services are emerging. The traditional "tailor moshai" market is ripe for disruption.
    49|
    50|7. **Size inclusivity gap** — Most brands offer S-XL only. Plus-size market (estimated 30%+ of women) is almost entirely unserved.

## Verified Market Data (2024–2025)

### Market Sizing
- Domestic fashion retail: **$34–36B USD** (separate from $38B export market)
- Domestic market includes: ready-made garments, traditional wear, accessories
- Online fashion: ~$2–3B subset, growing 25%+ annually

### Growth Drivers
- Population 170M+ with median age ~28
- Urbanization rate ~38% and rising
- Rising disposable income in middle class (~$5K+ annual household)
- Social media penetration driving fashion awareness
- E-commerce platforms making fashion accessible tier-2/3 cities

### Competitor Landscape Notes
- Aarong dominates organized retail (BRAC-backed, artisan positioning)
- Cats Eye leads premium menswear segment
- Yellow/Beximco targets younger demographic with trend-focused designs
- Many local brands (Ecstasy, Sada Kalo, Sailor) strong but smaller
- Facebook Commerce (F-commerce) is massive — thousands of small sellers

*Sources: LightCastle Partners 2024, BGMEA 2024, The Daily Star 2023*
