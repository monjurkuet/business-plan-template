## Key Trends

1. **D2C explosion via Instagram/Facebook** — New brands launch weekly with zero physical presence. The barrier to entry is a phone, a supplier, and a Facebook page.

2. **Modest fashion is massively underserved** — Bangladesh is a majority-Muslim country, yet few brands do modest fashion well beyond basic hijab lines. There's a gap for curated, trendy modest wear.

3. **Print-on-demand emerging** — Zero inventory model: customer orders → print → ship. Currently small but growing as local print partners emerge.

4. **Live commerce via Reels** — Facebook/Instagram Reels are becoming the primary product discovery channel. Brands that master live selling outperform static posters.

5. **Sustainable fashion niche** — Small but growing among affluent Gen-Z. Refashion, secondhand, and eco-friendly materials are gaining interest.

6. **Custom tailoring digitization** — 3D body scan apps and online measurement services are emerging. The traditional "tailor moshai" market is ripe for disruption.

7. **Size inclusivity gap** — Most brands offer S-XL only. Plus-size market (estimated 30%+ of women) is almost entirely unserved.

## Segment Analysis

### Streetwear / Urban
- **Size:** ~$1.5-2B
- **Growth:** 15-20% (fastest segment)
- **Players:** Ecstasy, Dorjee, plus 50+ micro-brands on Facebook
- **Opportunity:** Price bracket between Ecstasy (premium) and no-name (cheap) is wide open

### Modest Fashion
- **Size:** ~$3-4B (including hijab, abaya, modest casual)
- **Growth:** 10-12%
- **Players:** Lubnan, plus many small IG shops
- **Opportunity:** Massive demand, few quality players. Trendy modest = greenfield.

### Premium Basics
- **Size:** ~$500M-1B
- **Growth:** 20-25%
- **Players:** Labh, plus international brands (Uniqlo via resellers)
- **Opportunity:** Low brand count, high repeat-purchase rate, loyal customers

### Custom Tailoring
- **Size:** ~$2-3B (massive informal market)
- **Growth:** 5-8% (traditional), 30%+ (digitized segment)
- **Players:** No dominant digital player
- **Opportunity:** Entirely unorganized. Digital marketplace connecting tailors to customers could capture 15-20% commission on a $2B+ market.

## Strategic Recommendations for New Entrant

1. **Start with modest fashion + Instagram** — Biggest unserved demand, lowest competition, culturally aligned
2. **Use pre-order/drop model** — Zero inventory risk, cash upfront, produce to order
3. **Master Reels/TikTok content** — Product discovery happens here, not on websites
4. **Source locally** — BD garment manufacturing is world-class; use it for your own brand
5. **Build community, not just customers** — WhatsApp groups, exclusive drops, loyalty rewards
6. **Expand into plus-size** — 30%+ of women can't find clothes in BD brands — be the first to serve them
