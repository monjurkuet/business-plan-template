# Risk Register — Bangladesh Electronics & Gadgets

## Date: 2026-05-06

---

## Risk Assessment

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Score | Mitigation | Status |
|---|------|-----------|--------|-------|------------|--------|
| 1 | Refurbished phone quality issues | Medium | High | 🔴 High | Strict grading system. Replace any unit with defects within warranty. Limit initial volume to maintain quality. | Active |
| 2 | Customs/import regulation changes | Medium | High | 🔴 High | Diversify sourcing across Japan/US/UAE. Stay compliant with import duties. Build relationships with clearing agents. | Monitor |
| 3 | Daraz price undercutting | High | Low | 🟡 Medium | Compete on trust + curation, not price. Refurbished and rental are segments Daraz doesn't serve well. | Accept |
| 4 | Product returns/fraud | Medium | Medium | 🟡 Medium | Clear return policy (7-day for accessories, warranty for refurbished). Video evidence for high-value returns. | Active |
| 5 | Inventory cash flow pressure | Medium | High | 🔴 High | Start with low-inventory accessories. Refurbished phones: buy-to-order initially. Rental inventory generates recurring revenue. | Active |
| 6 | Tech content competition | High | Low | 🟢 Low | Bengali language + local market focus = differentiation. Most BD tech YouTubers review global products, not BD-specific buying advice. | Accept |

---

## Kill Criteria

- **Refurbished return rate >15%** → Quality problem, pause and improve grading
- **<50 accessory orders in first 60 days** → Product/market fit issue
- **YouTube channel <1,000 subscribers after 90 days** → Content strategy problem
- **Rental utilization <30%** → Not enough demand for rental segment
