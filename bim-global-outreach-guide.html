6 Outreach Framework

Pain-Point Messaging

Staffing Pressure: "We can't hire enough BIM/Revit staff fast enough"
Coordination Overload: "MEP clashes are killing our project timelines"
Documentation Overload: "We have 3 projects due and no bandwidth"
Fast-Track Deadlines: "We need LOD 400 models yesterday"
Margin Pressure: "Our margins are squeezed, we can't sacrifice quality"

Best Outreach Angle

"We help overloaded architecture and construction teams handle BIM production and MEP coordination during high-volume project phases."
Instead of: "We provide BIM services."

  • LinkedIn — Connection + InMail, 20% acceptance target

  • 3 consecutive months without a closed deal

15 Key Assumptions & Strategy Notes

Key Assumptions

  1. Target firms (20–500 emp) are overloaded enough to outsource
  2. Architecture + MEP expertise is a genuine differentiator
  3. US/UK/EU firms will trust BD-based team at comparable quality
  4. $50–150/hr rates achievable with consultative positioning
  5. Consultative outreach outperforms transaction-based pitching

Final Internal Note

Mass outreach works best when messaging is personalized, pain points are identified, and outreach feels consultative rather than sales-heavy.

The goal is not to "sell BIM."
The goal is to solve: staffing pressure, coordination problems, documentation overload, and project delivery bottlenecks.
